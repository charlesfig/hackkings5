New Chairman of Council

Baroness Rawlings is to be the new Chairman of the Council (governing body) of King's College London. She will succeed Sir James Spooner, who has served with great distinction as Chairman since 1986 and whose term of office ends on 31 July. She will be the first woman to chair the College's Council.

Baroness Rawlings (Patricia Rawlings) is 59. She was educated at Florence University, University College London (BA Hons) and at LSE (postgraduate diploma in International Relations). She was made a life peer in 1994 and sits on the Conservative Front Bench in the House of Lords, where she speaks for culture and foreign affairs.

Her current activities include:

  • Hon. Vice-President, British Red Cross Society
  • Council Member, Royal Institute of International Affairs (Chatham House)
  • Director, English Chamber Orchestra
  • Member, Advisory Council, The Prince's Youth Business Trust
  • Member, British Association for Eastern and Central Europe
  • Member, British Council

Her past activities include:

  • Member of the European Parliament for Essex South West, 1989-94
  • Member, Central Council for Education and Training in Social Work, 1995-6
  • Member, Council of the British Board of Video Classification, 1986-9
  • Special Adviser to the Minister for Inner Cities (Department of the Environment), 1987-8
  • Member, Council of Peace through NATO, 1986-8
  • Assessor for the Children's Care Committee of the London County Council, 1959-61.

Sir James Spooner commented: 'I am delighted that Patricia Rawlings is to succeed me as Chairman of King's Council. I have known her for many years and she will do an excellent job. As a result of the merger last year with the Institute of Psychiatry and the forthcoming merger in August with the United Medical and Dental Schools of Guy's and St Thomas' Hospitals (UMDS), a new King's is being created which will secure the College's place in the premier league of higher education institutions. I am very pleased that Baroness Rawlings will lead it forward into this exciting new stage of development.'

Baroness Rawlings said: 'I am thrilled to have been elected to be the new Chairman of the Council of King's. It is a tremendous honour to be associated with such a distinguished and historic college. It is a challenge to which I greatly look forward.'
